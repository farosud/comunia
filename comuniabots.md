# Comunia Bot Partnership Plan

Last updated: March 31, 2026

## Positioning

Comunia should not pitch itself as "another moderation bot" or "another support bot."

The strongest positioning is:

- Comunia is the community intelligence layer that turns conversations, support requests, behavior signals, and recurring asks into:
  - product ideas
  - community perks
  - retention plays
  - monetizable tools or services

That makes the best partner targets the bots that already own one of these workflows:

- moderation
- support
- analytics
- memberships/payments
- scheduling/events

## Telegram

### 1. InviteMember

Best fit if Comunia wants paid communities, memberships, and direct revenue impact.

Why it fits:

- InviteMember runs the membership/payment layer for Telegram businesses.
- It already supports customer chat, broadcasts, affiliate workflows, payments, and auto add/remove for groups and channels.
- InviteMember explicitly integrates with SUCH for support.

Contact surface:

- `@InviteMemberSupportBot`
- `support@invitemember.com`
- `@InviteMemberBot`

Partnership pitch:

Comunia can become the "community demand and roadmap layer" for InviteMember-powered businesses:

- detect recurring customer requests
- surface the next 10 things subscribers want
- propose community perks, tools, or digital products to build
- identify retention and upsell opportunities from chat/support data

Best angle:

- retention
- productized perks
- roadmap intelligence for paid communities

## Recommended strategy

### Highest-probability Telegram targets

1. InviteMember
2. SUCH
3. TeleMe

Reason:

- they are smaller, more partnership-shaped, and more likely to respond founder-to-founder

### Highest-probability Discord targets

1. CommunityOne
2. sesh
3. Statbot

Reason:

- they are strong product fits and less likely than MEE6 to require heavyweight BD motion

### Best first wedge for Comunia

Pitch a narrow first integration:

- "Top 10 things your community wants next"
- weekly opportunity digest
- product/perk recommendation board
- roadmap clustering from support/community conversations

That is easier to sell than:

- full AI community manager
- deep workflow replacement

## Caveat

I found strong evidence these products are active and servicing communities, with official product/contact surfaces.
I did not verify that all of them have formal public partner programs.

For most of these, the practical motion is likely:

- founder outreach
- support-team intro
- integration proposal
- pilot with one shared customer or one narrow feature
